Executive Summary

Project Overview

The Meridian Viaduct project represents a paradigm shift in seismic-resistant structural design. Spanning 1.2 kilometers across the industrial valley, the structure was conceptualized to serve as a vital lifeline for regional logistics while setting a new benchmark for structural longevity.

The primary goal was to create a resilient transport corridor capable of withstanding extreme environmental forces. By integrating a bespoke tuned mass damping system with a post-tensioned segmental concrete approach, the impact has been a 40% reduction in peak dynamic stress, ensuring safety for the next century of civic use.

The Engineering Journey

warning

Geotechnical Instability

The geotechnical survey revealed unexpected limestone cavities during the third phase of construction, threatening the stability of the central support pylons.

The Solution

We pivoted to a deep-pile foundation strategy, utilizing high-pressure grouting to stabilize the sub-surface without compromising the project timeline.

sensors

Thermal Expansion Drift

The extreme temperature fluctuations in the valley caused unpredictable thermal expansion, potentially impacting the precision of the segmental joints.

The Solution

Developed a custom fiber-optic sensor array embedded within the structural core to provide real-time thermal expansion data, allowing for dynamic joint adjustments.
